Understanding PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome affects 1 in 5 Indian women. It is manageable.

What is PCOS?

PCOS is a hormonal disorder where the ovaries produce excess male hormones (androgens). This can cause irregular periods, small cysts on ovaries, weight gain, acne, and excess hair growth.

Common Symptoms
  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Weight gain, especially around the waist
  • Acne and oily skin
  • Excess facial/body hair
  • Thinning scalp hair
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
What You Can Do
  • Maintain a healthy weight through diet and exercise
  • Limit sugar and processed foods
  • Exercise regularly — even a 30-minute walk helps
  • Manage stress through yoga or meditation
  • Consult a gynaecologist for proper diagnosis
⚠️ When to See a Doctor

If your cycle is consistently irregular (less than 21 or more than 35 days), you experience severe pain, or you have been trying to conceive for over 6 months.

Period Health Tips

Small habits, big difference

🍃
Eat iron-rich foods

Spinach, lentils, dates, and jaggery help replenish iron lost during menstruation.

💧
Stay hydrated

Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily. Herbal teas like ginger or chamomile help with cramps.

😴
Prioritise sleep

7–9 hours of sleep regulates hormones and reduces mood swings and fatigue.

🧘
Gentle movement

Light yoga, walking, or stretching can reduce cramps and improve blood flow during your period.

🚫
Avoid these

Reduce caffeine, alcohol, salty snacks, and cold drinks during your period — they can worsen bloating and cramps.

🌡️
Heat therapy

A warm compress or heating pad on your lower abdomen for 15–20 minutes can significantly reduce cramp intensity.

💬

Myths vs Facts

Know what's true about your cycle

MYTH

You should not exercise during your period.

FACT

Light exercise like yoga and walking actually helps reduce cramps and improves mood. Avoid only high-intensity workouts if you feel weak.

MYTH

Irregular periods are always a sign of something serious.

FACT

Occasional irregularity is common and can be caused by stress, travel, or diet changes. Persistent irregularity should be checked by a doctor.

MYTH

Period blood is dirty or impure.

FACT

Period blood is a mix of blood, uterine lining, and cervical mucus. It is a natural bodily process — there is nothing dirty about it.

MYTH

You can't get pregnant during your period.

FACT

While less likely, pregnancy during menstruation is possible, especially with shorter cycles. Sperm can survive inside the body for up to 5 days.

MYTH

Painful periods are normal and you just have to bear it.

FACT

Mild discomfort is common, but severe pain is not normal. Conditions like endometriosis and fibroids can cause intense pain and should be diagnosed.

Indian Foods for Cycle Health

Your kitchen is your medicine cabinet

🌿
Methi (Fenugreek)

Reduces menstrual cramps and regulates blood sugar — helpful for PCOS.

🧡
Haldi (Turmeric)

Anti-inflammatory properties reduce bloating and pain. Best in warm milk.

🫚
Til (Sesame seeds)

Rich in iron and calcium. Til ke ladoo before periods helps build strength.

🟫
Jaggery (Gur)

Natural iron source that replenishes blood loss. Better than refined sugar.

🍃
Spinach (Palak)

High in iron, magnesium, and folate — essential during and after menstruation.

🫘
Dal (Lentils)

Protein and iron-rich. Masoor dal and moong dal are excellent choices daily.

🥥
Coconut water

Natural electrolytes that reduce bloating, headaches, and fatigue during period.

🫚
Ajwain (Carom seeds)

Warm ajwain water relieves period cramps quickly — a trusted home remedy.

Hormones & Your Mood

Understanding why you feel what you feel

Menstrual (Day 1–5)

Estrogen and progesterone are at their lowest. You may feel tired, emotional, and have low energy. This is completely normal — rest and nourish yourself.

Follicular (Day 6–13)

Estrogen rises. Energy returns, mood improves, and you feel more motivated and social. A great time for new projects and challenges.

Ovulation (Day 14–17)

Estrogen peaks, then LH surges. You feel your most confident and social. Energy is highest. Some women feel mild mid-cycle pain (mittelschmerz).

Luteal (Day 18–28)

Progesterone rises, then falls. PMS symptoms — bloating, mood swings, cravings, and irritability — can occur in the last few days. Self-care is especially important now.
